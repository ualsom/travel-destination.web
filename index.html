    Kathmandu Durbar Square,

    a UNESCO World Heritage Site, was constructed over several centuries,
    primarily during the Malla Dynasty (12th to 18th centuries). It served as the royal palace and the heart of the city. real picture
    **Key Features:**
    1. **Hanuman Dhoka Palace**: The former royal palace of the Malla kings, known for its intricate woodwork.
    2. **Kastamandap**: A historic temple built from the wood of a single tree, believed to be the origin of Kathmandu's name.

    Swayambhunath


    Swayambhunath, also known as the Monkey Temple, is one of the oldest and most significant religious sites in Kathmandu, Nepal.
    This UNESCO World Heritage Site is perched atop a hill, offering panoramic views of the city.

    ### Key Features:


    **Stupa**: The stupa features a large dome topped by a golden spire adorned with Buddhaâ€™s eyes and a third eye, symbolizing wisdom.
    **Monkeys**: The site is known for the many monkeys that inhabit the area, adding to its lively atmosphere.
    **Temples and Shrines**: Surrounding the stupa are various shrines, temples, and prayer wheels, representing both Buddhist and Hindu traditions.
    **Cultural Significance**: Swayambhunath is a pilgrimage site for both Buddhists and Hindus and plays a vital role in the religious landscape of Nepal.
    Visiting Swayambhunath offers a unique blend of spirituality, history, and breathtaking views, making it a must-see destination.
